PHASE 1: Opening & Role Context (10-15 min)

Starter Question

"Tell me about your journey to Workday and your current role?"

Branch A: If they emphasize excitement about technology/their work

  • "That's great! What does your role actually encompass day-to-day?"
  • "How big is your team, and what do you own end-to-end?"
  • "What are you most excited about technically right now?"

Branch B: If they mention organizational change/challenges

  • "How has that affected your team specifically?"
  • "What's changed as a result?"
  • "How's the team handling it?"

Branch C: If they talk about team/office location

  • "Does your team own products end-to-end or support other teams?"
  • "What's the decision-making autonomy like?"
  • "How does your location fit into the broader organization?"

PHASE 2: Technical Reality Check (10-15 min)

Starter Question

"I'm trying to understand day-to-day engineering at Workday. How often does your team deploy to production?"

Branch A: If "daily" or "multiple times per day"

  • "That's impressive! What CI/CD setup enables that velocity?"
  • "Do teams ship independently or coordinate releases?"
  • "What DORA metrics do you track?"
  • Then: "Does Workday provide AI coding tools like Copilot or Cursor?"

Branch B: If "weekly" or "sprint-based"

  • "Is that driven by customer validation needs or internal process?"
  • "How does that cadence affect iteration and experimentation?"
  • "Are there initiatives to speed that up?"
  • Then: "What productivity tools does engineering get?"

Branch C: If vague or "it depends"

  • "Walk me through a recent feature - from commit to production."
  • "What's the biggest bottleneck in your deployment pipeline?"
  • "Any technical debt or legacy constraints slowing you down?"

PHASE 3: Culture & Leadership (10 min)

Leadership Question

"How would you describe the leadership and culture at Workday?"

If they critique leadership

  • "What specifically is frustrating people?"
  • "Do engineers feel heard and valued by leadership?"
  • "Is there trust in the strategic direction?"

If they defend leadership

  • "What are leaders doing well?"
  • "How do they communicate strategy and vision?"
  • "Do engineers have a seat at the strategic table?"

Team Dynamics

"Is technology positioned as a strategic partner or more of an execution arm?"
  • "Do engineers drive product decisions or mostly implement?"
  • "Who owns technical architecture decisions?"
  • "How is the eng/product relationship?"

PHASE 4: Work Experience & Sustainability (10 min)

Day-to-Day Experience

"What's the actual day-to-day experience like at Workday?"
  • "What are typical working hours for senior ICs?"
  • "Any on-call burden or production support expectations?"
  • "How's work-life balance in practice?"
  • "Are there clear career paths on the IC track?"

Compensation (if comfortable)

"What does typical total comp look like for a senior engineer - base, equity, bonus?"
  • "How competitive is it compared to other companies?"
  • "What's the equity/refresh structure?"
  • "What's attrition like? Who are you competing with for talent?"

Remote/Hybrid Policies

  • "How is [their hybrid policy] enforced in practice?"
  • "Is it flexible or bureaucratic?"
  • "How do people feel about it?"

PHASE 5: Honest Reflection (5-10 min)

Question 1: Biggest Frustration

"Real talk - what's the biggest frustration or challenge working at Workday right now?"

Listen for: bureaucracy, slow decisions, morale, comp, tech debt, autonomy

Question 2: One Change

"If you could change one thing about the engineering culture or organization, what would it be?"

Listen for: process, tooling, leadership, team structure, policies

Question 3: Why Stay?

"Why are you still at Workday? What keeps you engaged?"

Listen for: mission, people, comp, growth, technical work, stability

Question 4: Would You Join Today?

"Last one - would you join Workday today knowing what you know now?"
  • If "yes": "What would seal the deal if you were evaluating it today?"
  • If hesitate or "no": "What would need to change for it to be a 'hell yes'?"
