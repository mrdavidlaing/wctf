**Alternative Interpretation:**
Roxanne may have been referring to:
1. **Spotify's multiple layoffs in 2023** (not Chronosphere's) - she mentioned Spotify had waves
2. **The ongoing nature of July 2023 reductions** - possibly multiple tranches within that event
3. **Confusion about timing** - she was stressed, new, and may have misremembered

### 2. **Unreported Small Reduction** (Possible but Unlikely)

Could there have been a small, targeted reduction that didn't trigger public reporting?

**Against this theory:**
- Sales dysfunction reviews mention HIGH VOLUNTARY turnover ("how many reps have left on their own")
- Voluntary departures wouldn't be described as "layoff"
- Any meaningful reduction (even 10-20 people) would appear on Blind or Glassdoor
- Headcount growth trajectory shows no dip

**For this theory:**
- Very small reductions (<10 people) might not be reported
- Could have been performance-based terminations framed differently

**Verdict:** Unlikely - voluntary turnover more consistent with evidence

### 3. **Roxanne's Perspective as Designer** (Context Matters)

Roxanne joined in design org, which is small. What feels like "another layoff" to a small team might be:
- A few design team departures (voluntary or performance)
- Restructuring within her specific department
- Her interpreting sales org voluntary turnover as "layoffs"

She also said: "But actually, there was another layoff" somewhat **casually** while discussing company size, not as a major concern. This suggests:
- Not a major event (or she'd have elaborated)
- Possibly misremembering or conflating events
- May have been repeating company rumor without verification
